Food Porter — Complete User Handbook

A screen-by-screen guide to the whole platform, for everyone who uses it: donors who share surplus food, volunteers who help distribute it, drivers who run the deliveries, and foundation staff who coordinate it all.

How the rescue works. A donor posts surplus food. The foundation's driver collects it, and volunteers ride along as helping hands to distribute it to people in need. Staff coordinate pickups into delivery runs and watch everything live on one dashboard.

Section 1

For everyone

Whoever you are, a few things work the same across the whole app.

The four roles

RoleWho they areWhat they do in the app

Signing in

  • Donors, volunteers, drivers: choose Sign in or Become a volunteer, then enter the code we email you — no password to remember.
  • Foundation staff: use the Staff sign-in page (footer link, or /staff).

The top bar

  • EN · ગુ · हि — switch language (English, Gujarati, Hindi) any time; the whole app follows.
  • Moon / Sun — light or dark theme.
  • Your avatar — account menu and sign-out.

Notifications

  • Phone / browser push (the main channel) — tap Enable notifications and choose Allow. This is what lets alerts reach your phone when the app is closed.
  • Email (secondary) — receipts and some updates.
Not getting alerts? The usual reason is that "Allow notifications" was never tapped in the browser. Push only works after you allow it.

Section 3 · Donor

Donor guide

You have surplus food — a restaurant tray, a wedding's extra thalis, a hostel's leftovers. Here's how to get it to people who need it, in about a minute.

Your dashboard

  • Total posted · Open · In progress · Delivered — your tally at a glance.
  • Post a pickup — start here to share food.
  • Recent pickups — your latest posts and their status.

Posting a pickup — step by step

  1. Food type — cooked meal, bakery, packaged, raw produce…
  2. Quantity + Unit — how much, in servings or kg.
  3. Description (optional) — e.g. "40 veg thalis from a wedding."
  4. When to pick up — the from and until times. Choose a realistic window; food safety depends on it.
  5. Pickup address — paste a Google Maps link or type an area/landmark, then tap Find to drop the pin.
  6. Food photo (optional) — helps, and is nice for the records.
  7. Confirm the food is fresh and safe, then Post pickup.
Food-safety promise. The confirmation checkbox is a real commitment: only post food that is fresh and safe to eat at pickup time.

My pickups & tracking

Open any pickup to see its details and, once it's on the way, track it live on a map. When it turns Delivered, your food reached someone. You can edit or cancel a pickup only while it's still Requested — once claimed, it's in motion.

Section 4 · Volunteer

Volunteer guide

You help get rescued food to people who need it. Here's your side of the app.

Your dashboard

The pickup board

A card per pickup shows the food type, quantity, time window, and area, with a Requested pill until it's collected. A counter shows how many are waiting.

A pickup up close

Claiming assigns the pickup so two people never turn up for the same food. Once active, it moves through accepted → en route → picked up → delivered, and the donor can watch it live. Volunteers ride with the driver to hand out the food and confirm the drop.

Tip: if you can't collect it after all, don't claim it — leave it for someone who can.

Section 5 · Driver

Driver guide

You collect the food and run the delivery route. The app gives you today's run and lets you tick off each stop.

My Run

My Run is your working checklist for the shift:

  • Each stop in order — a Pickup to collect or a Drop to deliver, with its area and a Pending marker until done.
  • Mark done — tap it as you finish each stop; the run auto-completes when every stop is done.
  • Sharing your location — while the run is active, your live location shows on the map for coordinators and donors, and is deleted the moment you mark delivered. Keep your screen on during the run.
  • Volunteers riding with you can also confirm drops.

Before a run is assigned, this screen reads "No run assigned to you yet — check back later," with the coordinator's number.

The structure of a run — its ordered pickup and drop stops — is the same one staff build; see the run detail screen in the admin guide.

Section 6 · Staff

Admin & staff guide

The coordinator's cockpit. Everything the foundation needs to run the operation lives under Staff sign-in → Admin. This section is your training and support reference. The left sidebar is your map: Overview · Pickups · Dispatch Runs · Destinations · Partners · Users · Reports.

Overview

  • Top-line tiles — meals & kg rescued, deliveries, open pickups, in-progress, active runs.
  • Deliveries (30 days), Pickup status donut, Top partners & destinations, and directory counts.
Use it to spot a pile-up of Open pickups that need dispatching, or In progress items that have stalled.

Pickups

What to do when: a pickup sits Open too long → assign it (or it auto-cancels after its window lapses). Need a specific person on it → open and assign the volunteer/driver.

Dispatch Runs

A run is a driver's route — a set of pickup and drop stops done in one trip.

Building a new run

Assigning a driver notifies them.

Inside a run

Reorder, add, or remove stops, override a stop's status, and follow the driver's progress. This is exactly what the driver sees as their checklist.

Destinations

Shelters, night zones, community points. They become the drop stops you choose when building runs.

Partners

Linking a donor to a partner lets the foundation attribute rescued food to that partner in reports.

Users

Inviting a user takes their name (plus optional phone/city) up front — invited users skip onboarding entirely and land straight on their dashboard after signing in via the emailed link; their profile is provisioned automatically from what the admin entered.

What to do when: onboard a coordinator → invite by email with the admin role, they sign in via the link with no onboarding step. Someone should lose access → deactivate them (immediate). Safeguards prevent removing the last admin.

Reports

  • Meals / kg rescued and deliveries for the period.
  • Average and 90%-within rescue time (posted → delivered) — the food-safety and impact headline.
  • Breakdowns by run, destination, and partner, with CSV export.

Appendix

The rescue loop

1 · PostDonor posts surplus food
2 · DispatchStaff build it into a run
3 · PickupDriver collects the food
4 · DistributeDriver + volunteers deliver
5 · DeliveredReached people in need ✓

Every step is recorded with who did it and when. Nothing is marked delivered without going through the proper stages.

Pickup statuses

StatusMeaning
RequestedPosted, waiting to be collected (donor can still edit/cancel)
AcceptedClaimed/assigned — someone is on it
En routeOn the way to collect
Picked upFood collected, heading to the destination
DeliveredReached people in need — done
CancelledCalled off, or auto-cancelled after its window lapsed
