Food Porter — Complete User Handbook

A screen-by-screen guide to the whole platform, for everyone who uses it: donors who share surplus food, volunteers who help distribute it, drivers who collect the food and run the deliveries, and foundation staff who coordinate it all.

How the rescue works. A donor posts surplus food. The foundation's driver collects it — claimed from a board or assigned by staff — and volunteers never collect; they ride along or meet the run at the drop to help distribute it to people in need. Staff coordinate pickups into delivery runs, adjust them live, and watch everything on one dashboard.

Section 1

For everyone

Whoever you are, a few things work the same across the whole app.

The four roles

RoleWho they areWhat they do in the app
DonorA restaurant, hostel, family, or event host with surplus foodPosts food for pickup; tracks it to delivery
VolunteerA helper who assists with distributionBrowses for awareness only; joins active runs to confirm drops — never claims or collects
DriverThe foundation's driverClaims or is assigned pickups; runs the collection & delivery route; shares live location while active
Admin (staff)Foundation coordinatorsCoordinate pickups, build & adjust runs, manage people & places, read reports

Your role is set once, the first time you sign in. Admin access is granted by the foundation via an invite — you can't choose it yourself at sign-up.

Signing in

  • Donors, volunteers, drivers: choose Sign in or Become a volunteer (or the donor/driver equivalent), then enter the one-time code we email you — no password to remember.
  • Foundation staff: use the Staff sign-in page (footer link, or /staff) — a separately branded front door over the same account system; your admin role, not the door you used, is what actually unlocks the admin area.

Onboarding (first sign-in only)

  1. Pick a role — three cards: Donate food ("I have surplus food to share"), Volunteer ("I'll help distribute food"), Driver ("I drive collections & deliveries"). Not a dropdown — tap a card. May arrive pre-selected from a link.
  2. Complete your profile — name (pre-filled from your account when available), phone (optional), city.
Admin-invited users skip the role picker. If staff invited you with the admin role already set, onboarding only asks for your profile and lands you on the admin dashboard — the three role cards never appear.

The top bar

  • EN · ગુ · हि — switch language (English, Gujarati, Hindi) any time; saved as a cookie and mirrored to your profile, so notification copy follows it too.
  • Moon / Sun — light or dark theme, instantly.
  • Your avatar — Clerk's own account menu and sign-out (not a custom app screen).
  • Bell — your notifications (below).

Language, theme, and the account menu also exist for anyone on the public site, before signing in.

Notifications

  • The notification bell — tap to open the feed: recent notifications, newest first, timestamped. Tap an unread one to mark it read; Mark all read clears the badge in one tap. An empty inbox shows a simple empty state instead of a blank list.
  • Phone / browser push (the main channel) — tap Enable notifications and choose Allow. This is what lets alerts reach your phone when the app is closed. Shared by donor and volunteer dashboards; drivers land on My Run instead, so they don't currently see this card there.
  • Email (secondary) — receipts and some updates, once the foundation's email domain is fully set up.
Not getting alerts? The usual reason is that "Allow notifications" was never tapped in the browser. Push only works after you allow it.

Section 3 · Donor

Donor guide

You have surplus food — a restaurant tray, a wedding's extra thalis, a hostel's leftovers. Here's how to get it to people who need it, in about a minute.

Your dashboard

  • Total posted · Open · In progress · Delivered — your tally at a glance.
  • Post a pickup — start here to share food.
  • My pickups — a shortcut to everything you've posted.
  • Recent pickups — your latest posts and their status.
  • Enable notifications — push alerts for claims and delivery.

Posting a pickup

Tap Post a pickup — a sheet slides in with the "our driver will pick it up" copy, so the model is clear immediately. Three sections:

  1. Food type — cooked meal, bakery, packaged, raw produce…
  2. Quantity + Unit — how much, in servings or kg (never converted between the two — see the appendix).
  3. Description (optional) — e.g. "40 veg thalis from a wedding."
  4. Pickup window — from and until times. Choose a realistic window; food safety depends on it.
  5. Pickup address — paste a Google Maps link or type an area/landmark, tap Find — a spinner runs, then a draggable pin appears on a map below.
  6. Food photo (optional) — native camera picker, client-compressed, progress + checkmark.
  7. Safety checkbox — confirm the food is fresh and fit to share.
  8. Post pickup.
Food-safety promise. The confirmation checkbox is a real commitment: only post food that is fresh and safe to eat at pickup time.

My pickups

Editing, cancelling, reposting

  • Edit — shown only while Requested; opens the same form pre-filled in a sheet.
  • Cancel — also Requested-only, destructive, confirms first; purges any GPS trail as a side effect.
  • Repost — available at any status, no confirmation, navigates straight to the new pickup it creates.

Tracking a pickup live

While status is En route, the detail page swaps its static map for a live tracking map of the driver's position.

Your live view ends once the food is collected — deliberate leg-aware tracking (see the appendix). Status text keeps updating after the map view ends.

Status history (your receipt)

Scroll to History on a pickup's detail page: every status transition, timestamped, generated automatically — the closest thing to a receipt for the food itself.

Section 4 · Volunteer

Volunteer guide

You help get rescued food to people who need it — never by collecting it yourself. Here's your side of the app.

Your dashboard

The pickup board (read-only)

List/Map tabs, a card per open pickup. There is no claim button — a quiet note explains this screen is for awareness only. This is intentional (not a bug): dispatch-model-v2 removed the volunteer claim button, since collecting is the driver's job. Tap a card to view any active pickup's detail for awareness.

Today's Distributions

Runs (bottom nav) lists every currently active run and, for each, only its drop stops — the pickup leg is the driver's job and isn't shown here. Mark done confirms a drop; any volunteer can confirm one on an active run, not just an "assigned" volunteer, since whoever is physically present is the one who can see food handed over.

Tip: because you never claim anything, there's no "leave it for someone else" decision on the board — browse for awareness, act from Today's Distributions when a run is active.

Section 5 · Driver

Driver guide

You collect the food and run the delivery route. The app gives you a claim board, today's run, and lets you tick off each stop.

The pickup board

Claiming a pickup

Open any Requested pickup to reach Claim this pickup — atomic, so a second driver claiming the same food gets a clear "already taken" error and a refresh instead of a double booking.

Advancing status & proof photo

An "Advance to <next status>" button walks a claimed pickup through Accepted → En route → Picked up → Delivered — only you, the assigned driver, see it; it disappears at a terminal state. A proof-of-delivery photo upload appears only on the delivering step and is optional, not a gate — distribution is confirmed by whoever is present.

Navigate & share location

Navigate opens Google Maps with directions already loaded — a free deep link, no in-app dialog. Location sharing starts automatically (no button) while a pickup is en route/picked up or a run is active — a "Sharing your location…" banner confirms it, and the trail is deleted the moment you mark delivered. If location access is denied, a muted note explains sharing is off and the pickup still works normally.

My Run

  • Each stop in order — a Pickup to collect or a Drop to deliver, with a Pending marker until done.
  • Mark done — tap it as you finish each stop; the run auto-completes when every stop is done, purging your GPS trail.
  • Live map — route toward the next stop, road-routed when possible.
  • Volunteers riding with you can also confirm drops on the same run.

Before a run is assigned, this screen reads "No run assigned to you yet — check back later," with the coordinator's number.

The structure of a run — its ordered pickup and drop stops — is exactly what staff build; see Inside a run in the admin guide, including how a coordinator can reassign you mid-run or override a stop.

Section 6 · Staff

Admin & staff guide

The coordinator's cockpit. Everything the foundation needs to run the operation lives under Staff sign-in → Admin. This section is your training and support reference. The left sidebar is your map: Overview · Pickups · Dispatch Runs · Destinations · Partners · Users · Reports.

Overview

  • Top-line tiles — meals & kg rescued, deliveries, open pickups, in-progress, active runs.
  • Deliveries (30 days), Pickup status donut, Top partners & destinations, and directory counts.

How meals and kg are counted

A small "?" hint sits by each tile, because they're deliberately never converted into one another: Meals rescued = delivered pickups logged in servings (one serving = one meal); Kg rescued = delivered pickups logged by weight, counted completely separately. A pickup is logged in whichever unit was chosen at post time — never both.

Use Overview to spot a pile-up of Open pickups that need dispatching, or In progress items that look stalled.

Pickups

  • Filter & sort — status/date filters plus a sortable table; any change resets to page 1.
  • Assign a pickup to a driver — row menu → Assign, shown only on Requested rows; the picker is scoped to drivers only (assigning a volunteer here would leave the pickup stuck, since only the assigned driver role can advance it).
  • View a pickup — the same detail page everyone uses, with an admin-only Verify / Unverify toggle (bookkeeping only, never blocks dispatch).
  • Export CSV — carries the active filters.

What to do when: a pickup sits Open too long → assign it to a driver (or a nightly job auto-cancels it once its window lapses). Need a specific person → open it and assign a driver.

Dispatch Runs

A run is a driver's route — a set of pickup and drop stops done in one trip.

Creating a new run

Choose the slot and date, optionally pick a driver right away (or leave it for later). Stops aren't added here — you land on the new run's detail page to add them.

Inside a run

  • Advance / cancel the run via status buttons (legal transitions only); Cancel and Delete confirm first.
  • Delete — only while Planned or Cancelled.
  • Edit run button — a sheet for slot + date only (see below); driver is separate.
  • Assign / reassign the driver — a dedicated picker + Save, shown while the run is editable — this is how you change a run's driver after it exists.
  • Live map — appears once the run is Active.
  • Stops — reorder with up/down arrows, override a stop's status via an admin-only dropdown (Pending/Done/Skipped — a deliberate escape hatch for correcting a missed mark-done in the field), or remove a stop (confirms first). All editable only while Planned/Active.
  • Add a stop — Pickup/Drop toggle; pickups pick a partner, drops pick a saved destination or a free-text ad-hoc address (geocoded automatically).

Edit-run sheet

Destinations

Shelters, night zones, community points; they become the drop stops chosen when building runs. Delete is blocked with a conflict message if the destination was used by a past run.

Known limitation: the delete-conflict message suggests marking a destination inactive instead — but no such toggle exists anywhere in the UI today. There's no way to hide a destination from the picker short of deleting it (which the conflict guard may block).

Partners

Add/edit/delete partners (delete blocked if linked to donors or past run stops). Link a donor to a partner — a separate card with two plain dropdowns — attributes a donor's posts to that partner in reports.

Users

  • Change role — the row's dropdown fires immediately, no confirm dialog; your own row shows a static badge; the server blocks demoting the last active admin.
  • Invite a user — email + preset role; selecting Admin shows a hint — this is how you make someone an admin, there's no separate "promote" control.
  • Deactivate — confirms first; blocked on the last admin or your own account.
  • Reactivate — shown only on deactivated rows, fires immediately, no confirm (asymmetric on purpose).

Reports

  • Meals / kg rescued and deliveries for the period — same never-converted split as Overview.
  • Average and 90%-within rescue time (posted → delivered) — the food-safety and impact headline.
  • Date range — inline inputs, applies via shareable URL params.
  • Breakdowns by run, destination, and partner, with CSV export (runs, and pickups carrying the current filters).

Log surplus

For food that came in by phone or in person rather than a partner posting it themselves — tagged "logged by a coordinator" so it's distinguishable in reports.

Section 7

Appendix

The rescue loop (corrected model)

1 · PostDonor posts surplus food
2 · DispatchStaff build it into a run
3 · PickupDriver claims/is assigned it, collects
4 · DistributeDriver + volunteers deliver
5 · DeliveredReached people in need ✓

A driver collects — by claiming an open pickup or being assigned into a run. Volunteers are never the collector; they ride along or meet the run at the drop to help distribute and confirm it. Staff build runs, adjust them live, and can manually assign a specific driver outside the normal claim flow. Every step is recorded — who did it and when — via an audited status-event trail; nothing is marked delivered without going through the proper stages.
