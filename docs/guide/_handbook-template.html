Rajyash Foundation · Food Rescue

Food Porter — Complete User Handbook

Every action, for everyone who uses the platform: donors who share surplus food, volunteers who help distribute it, drivers who collect the food and run the deliveries, and foundation staff who coordinate it all. Each persona section opens with a "what you can do" powers table, then covers every action end to end.

How the rescue works. A donor posts surplus food. The foundation's driver collects it — claimed from a board or assigned by staff — and volunteers never collect; they ride along or meet the run at the drop to help distribute it to people in need. Staff coordinate pickups into delivery runs, adjust them live, and watch everything on one dashboard.

Signing in

  • Donors, volunteers, drivers: choose Sign in or Become a volunteer (or the donor/driver equivalent), then enter the one-time code we email you — no password to remember. Google sign-in is also offered.
  • Foundation staff: use the Staff sign-in page (footer link, or /staff) — a separately branded front door over the same account system; your admin role, not the door you used, is what actually unlocks the admin area — the server re-checks it on every admin request.

Onboarding (first sign-in only)

Where it lives: /onboarding, shown automatically the first time a self-registered user signs in.

  1. Pick a role — three cards: Donate food ("I have surplus food to share"), Volunteer ("I'll help distribute food"), Driver ("I drive collections & deliveries"). Not a dropdown — tap a card. May arrive pre-selected from a link.
  2. Complete your profile — name (pre-filled from your account when available), phone (optional), city.
  3. Submit — your account metadata and a database profile row are written together, and you land on your home screen (/portal/dashboard, or /portal/run for drivers).
Who can do this / boundary. Every self-registered user passes through here exactly once; your role is re-validated server-side regardless of what the client sends — you can never self-assign admin from this screen.
Invited users never see onboarding. If staff invited you (any role), your name, role, and city were set at invite time — your first sign-in provisions your profile automatically and lands you straight on your dashboard (admins on the admin dashboard). The three role cards never appear; a small dead-code fallback for this case still exists on the page but is unreachable in practice.

The top bar

  • EN · ગુ · हि — switch language (English, Gujarati, Hindi) any time; saved as a cookie and mirrored to your profile, so notification copy follows it too.
  • Moon / Sun — light or dark theme, instantly, no reload.
  • Your avatar — Clerk's own account menu and sign-out (not a custom app screen).
  • Bell — your notifications (below).

These four controls have no role gate at all — available to every signed-in user, on every persona. Language, theme, and the account menu also exist for anyone on the public site, before signing in.

Notifications

  • The notification bell — tap to open the feed: recent notifications, newest first, timestamped. Tap an unread one to mark it read; Mark all read clears the badge in one tap. An empty inbox shows a simple empty state instead of a blank list.
  • Phone / browser push (the main channel) — tap Enable notifications and choose Allow. This is what lets alerts reach your phone when the app is closed. The opt-in card now appears on the donor dashboard, the volunteer dashboard, and the driver's My Run page — every persona has a discoverable place to turn it on.
  • Email (secondary) — receipts and some updates, once the foundation's email domain is fully set up.
Who can do this / boundary. Any signed-in user; marking a notification read is scoped to your own user id server-side — you can never mark or see someone else's by guessing an id.
Not getting alerts? The usual reason is that "Allow notifications" was never tapped in the browser. Push only works after you allow it.

Your dashboard

  • Total posted · Open · In progress · Delivered — your tally at a glance.
  • Post a pickup — start here to share food.
  • My pickups — a shortcut to everything you've posted.
  • Recent pickups — your latest posts and their status.

Posting a pickup

Where it lives: tap Post a pickup on the dashboard, or the ＋ on My Pickups — a sheet slides in with the "our driver will pick it up" copy.

Fields, step by step:

  1. Food type — cooked meal, bakery, packaged, raw produce…
  2. Quantity + Unit — servings or kg (never converted between the two — see the appendix).
  3. Description (optional) — e.g. "40 veg thalis from a wedding."
  4. Pickup window — from and until times. Choose a realistic window; food safety depends on it.
  5. Pickup address — paste a Google Maps link or type an area/landmark, tap Find — a spinner runs, then a draggable pin appears on a map below (see Find address on map below).
  6. Food photo (optional) — native camera picker, client-compressed, progress + checkmark.
  7. Safety checkbox — confirm the food is fresh and fit to share.
  8. Post pickup.

What happens on submit: a new pickup is created in Requested status, attributed to you. What you see next: the sheet closes and it appears at the top of My Pickups, now on the driver board.

Who can do this / boundary. Donors only; if the foundation's new-pickup intake kill switch is off, posting is blocked server-side too, not just hidden in the UI.
Food-safety promise. The confirmation checkbox is a real commitment: only post food that is fresh and safe to eat at pickup time.

Repeat last pickup

Where it lives: a "Repeat last pickup" button at the top of the post-pickup sheet, shown only once you have at least one prior pickup. How you do it: open the sheet, tap the button. What happens: category, quantity, unit, address, pin, and any Maps link prefill from your most recent pickup — your window and photo deliberately do not carry over, since those should always be set fresh.

Who can do this / boundary. Donors only; "last pickup" is always pulled from your own posting history — the lookup is scoped to your session id server-side, never a client-supplied one.

Find address on map

Where it lives: the Find button next to the address field, in both the post and edit forms. How you do it: paste a Google Maps share link, or type a plain address, then tap Find. What happens: a brief spinner runs, then a map appears with a pin dropped at the resolved location — drag it to fine-tune. The pin's coordinates are what get saved on submit.

Upload food photo

Where it lives: Add photo in the post form (optional). How you do it: tap it — your device's camera/file picker opens. What happens: the image compresses on-device, uploads with a progress bar, and a checkmark confirms it finished — visible to whoever later views the pickup's detail page.

My pickups

Who can do this / boundary. Scoped to your own donor id server-side — you never see another donor's pickups here.

Editing, cancelling, reposting

  • Edit — shown only while Requested; opens the same form pre-filled in a sheet, closes/refreshes in place on submit.
  • Cancel — also Requested-only, destructive, confirms first; purges any GPS trail as a side effect.
  • Repost — available at any status, no confirmation, fires immediately and navigates straight to the new pickup it creates.
Who can do this / boundary. Edit and Cancel both re-check your ownership and the pickup's current status server-side — either check failing returns a conflict error, not a silent no-op. Repost explicitly checks the source pickup's donor id matches you.

Tracking a pickup live

While status is En route, the detail page automatically swaps its static map for a live tracking map of the driver's position, updating roughly every 30 seconds.

Your live view ends once the food is collected — deliberate leg-aware tracking (see the appendix). Status text keeps updating after the map view ends.

Status timeline (history)

The History section on your pickup's detail page shows all four stages as a vertical timeline, current stage highlighted, timestamps shown for stages reached — but no actor names to donors (privacy). Any other viewer (admin, or a driver/volunteer browsing) instead sees a plain flat → status · time list of the same underlying data.

Delivery proof-back

Where it lives: a pickup's detail page, automatically, once it's Delivered and the driver attached a proof photo. What you see: "Your food reached families in need" plus the driver's proof photo. (No screenshot exists — no seed pickup currently has both conditions true; described here from the underlying behaviour.)

Note on wording. The copy is deliberately generic, not destination-named — a pickup has no direct link to the exact destination it's dropped at (that link is one hop away, at the run/stop level).

You never claim or collect a pickup. There is no claim button anywhere in your view of the app — that control only renders for drivers.

Your dashboard

Where it lives: /portal/dashboard. It leads with distribution stats and a prominent CTA; the read-only board is demoted to a smaller "what's cooking" link further down — a deliberate rebalance of an existing page, not a new route.

The pickup board (read-only)

Where it lives: bottom nav → Board. List/Map tabs, a card per open pickup. There is no claim button — a quiet note explains this screen is for awareness only. Tap a card to view any active pickup's detail.

Who can do this / boundary. Intentional, not a bug: dispatch-model-v2 removed the volunteer claim button, since collecting is the driver's job. The claim control's code only renders for the driver role.

Today's Distributions

Where it lives: bottom nav → Runs (/portal/distributions). Lists every currently active run's drop stops only — the pickup leg is the driver's job and isn't shown here. A map of the drop-stop pins renders automatically once at least one stop has coordinates.

Confirm a drop: tap Mark done on a stop once you've physically handed food over. If it was the run's last stop, the run auto-completes and the toast says so.

Who can do this / boundary. Any volunteer can confirm a drop on any active run — not restricted to one "assigned" volunteer, since whoever is physically present is who can see food handed over. If no run is active, a plain empty state shows instead of an empty list.
Tip: because you never claim anything, there's no "leave it for someone else" decision on the board — browse for awareness, act from Today's Distributions when a run is active.

The pickup board

Where it lives: bottom nav → Board — same list/map view as volunteers, plus a Claim control. A distance chip sits next to each pickup's status pill and the board re-sorts nearest-first automatically.

Who can do this / boundary. The distance sort is a client-only convenience — a one-shot device-location request, no server call — and silently falls back to chronological order if you deny access or it times out.

Claiming a pickup

Two entry points call the exact same action: from the board card (tap Claim → confirm in a two-tap dialog) or from the pickup's detail page (a large "Claim this pickup" button). Either way the claim is atomic: a second driver claiming the same pickup a split second later gets a clear "already taken" error and a refresh, instead of a double booking.

Who can do this / boundary. Drivers only; claim only succeeds if the pickup is still unclaimed at that exact instant — no window for two drivers to both "win."

See distance to each open pickup

Covered above under The pickup board — tracked as its own action since it's a distinct piece of driver-only UX.

Call the donor / navigate

Call — a plain tel: link on the pickup detail page and each run stop; tapping it opens your phone's dialer pre-filled. Navigate — opens Google Maps in a new tab with turn-by-turn directions already loaded, no in-app dialog, no server call.

Who can do this / boundary. The phone number is only ever shown to you, the assigned driver on that pickup — anyone else (a browsing volunteer, or you before claiming) never sees it; if there's simply no phone on file, the button renders nothing rather than a broken control.

Advancing status & proof photo

An "Advance to <next status>" button walks a claimed pickup through Accepted → En route → Picked up → Delivered — on mobile it becomes a fixed bottom bar, always thumb-reachable above the bottom nav; only you, the assigned driver, see it, and it disappears at a terminal state. A proof-of-delivery photo upload appears only on the delivering step and is optional, not a gate — you can advance to Delivered without one.

Who can do this / boundary. The server checks the pickup's assigned-driver id against you before accepting an advance or a proof photo.

Share your live location

No button — starts automatically while a pickup is en route/picked up, or a run is active. A "Sharing your location…" banner confirms it; it self-stops the moment the server confirms the pickup/run is no longer active, and the trail is deleted the moment you mark delivered. If location access is denied, a muted note explains sharing is off and the pickup still works normally.

My Run

Where it lives: bottom nav → My run (/portal/run). Current-stop hero card up top, quieter remaining-stops list below. The Enable notifications card now appears here too — both on the empty state and with an active run — giving drivers a discoverable place to opt into push, since they land on this page instead of the shared dashboard.

  • Each stop in order — a Pickup to collect or a Drop to deliver, with a Pending marker until done.
  • Mark done — tap it as you finish each stop; the run auto-completes when every stop is done, purging your GPS trail.
  • Live map — route toward the next stop, road-routed when possible, straight-line + ETA fallback otherwise.
  • Volunteers riding with you can also confirm drops on the same run, from their own screen.
Who can do this / boundary. You see only your own active/next-planned run; marking a stop done here checks the run's driver id matches you. The live map is viewable by the run's driver, any admin, or any volunteer.

Before a run is assigned, this screen reads "No run assigned to you yet — check back later," with the coordinator's number, alongside the push opt-in card.

The structure of a run — its ordered pickup and drop stops — is exactly what staff build; see Inside a run in the admin guide, including how a coordinator can reassign you mid-run or override a stop.

Overview

Note on load time. Every aggregate query is already batched into one round trip rather than several, but the page can still occasionally feel slow to first paint on this hosting platform's serverless database connections. If it seems to hang, give it a moment before assuming something's broken — see Known limitations.

How meals and kg are counted

A small "?" hint sits by each tile, because they're deliberately never converted into one another: Meals rescued = delivered pickups logged in servings (one serving = one meal); Kg rescued = delivered pickups logged by weight, counted completely separately. A pickup is logged in whichever unit was chosen at post time — never both.

Use Overview to spot a pile-up of Open pickups that need dispatching, or In progress items that look stalled.

Log surplus on a partner's behalf

Where it lives: Log surplus header button (Overview or Pickups). Step by step: tap it → pick the partner first → fill the same food form a donor would (type, quantity/unit, description, window, address + Find) → submit. What happens on submit: a pickup is created, tagged logged by a coordinator so it's distinguishable in reports; you land on the Pickups list with the sheet closed.

Dispatch Runs

A run is a driver's route — a set of pickup and drop stops done in one trip. Tap a row's chevron to open its detail page.

Creating a new run

Where it lives: New run header button. Step by step: choose the slot (morning/night) and date → optionally pick a driver, or leave it for later → submit. Stops aren't added here — you land on the new run's detail page to add them.

Inside a run

  • Assign / reassign the driver — a dedicated picker + Save, shown while the run is editable — this is how you change a run's driver after it exists, separate from picking one at creation.
  • Edit run — a sheet for slot + date only; driver stays untouched, changed only via the control above.
  • Add a stop — Pickup/Drop toggle; pickups pick a partner, drops pick a saved destination (filtered to active-only) or a free-text ad-hoc address, geocoded automatically on submit.
  • Reorder stops — up/down arrows swap a stop with its neighbour immediately.
  • Override a stop's status — an admin-only dropdown directly on the stop row lets you force Pending/Done/Skipped, bypassing the normal driver/volunteer confirm flow — a deliberate escape hatch for a missed "mark done" in the field; rejected if the run is already Completed/Cancelled. Every override is recorded in the stop's audit trail below.
  • Remove a stop — trash icon, confirms first.
  • Advance / cancel the run — status buttons for legal transitions only; Cancel and Delete confirm first.
  • Delete a run — only while Planned or Cancelled.
  • Live map — appears once the run is Active.
  • View run stop history — a "History" collapsible section: a chronological <from> → <to> · actor · time (IST) line per stop, including admin overrides. A stop with zero recorded events (pre-dating this feature) falls back to a completion-time-only line with no actor — see Known limitations.

All stop editing (add/reorder/override/remove) is only available while the run is Planned or Active — a completed or cancelled run's stop list is permanently read-only.

Reports

Destinations

Shelters, night zones, community points; they become the drop stops chosen when building runs. Add a destination via a sheet (name, area, address + Find, pin, city) — always created active: true. Edit the same sheet, pre-filled, from the row menu.

Toggle active/inactive — inside the Edit sheet only (not Add): an Active switch. Flip it and submit — the list shows an Inactive badge, and a run's add-stop picker filters to active destinations only, so an inactive place quietly stops being offered for new runs without deleting it.

Delete a destination — confirms first; blocked with a conflict message if used by a past run, which now correctly points at the active/inactive toggle as the alternative.

Who can do this / boundary. Edit-only by design — a brand-new destination is always active; the toggle only matters once it's already in use.

Partners

Add/edit/delete partners (delete blocked if linked to donors or past run stops). Link a donor to a partner — a separate card with two plain native <select> dropdowns (donor, then partner — a known cosmetic inconsistency, see Known limitations) — attributes a donor's posts to that partner in reports.

Users

Search / filter users — type into the search box (matches name/email) and/or pick a role from the filter dropdown; the table filters live via URL params.

  • Change role — the row's dropdown fires immediately, no confirm dialog; your own row shows a static badge; the database role write happens before the Clerk metadata update, so a partial failure never leaves them disagreeing; the server blocks demoting the last active admin.
  • Deactivate — confirms first; blocked on the last admin or your own account.
  • Reactivate — shown only on deactivated rows, fires immediately, no confirm (asymmetric on purpose — restoring access is the safer direction).

Invite a user — step by step: tap Invite → enter email (required) → the person's name (required) → pick a role from a dropdown offering all four, including Admin (a hint appears — this is the only way to make someone an admin, there's no separate "promote" control) → optional phone (validated server-side) and city → Send invite. What happens on submit: a Clerk email invite goes out carrying the role/name/phone/city; when the person signs in through it, they skip onboarding entirely — their profile is provisioned automatically and they land straight on their dashboard. What you see next: an inline "invite sent" confirmation (not a toast), so you can invite the next person without losing your place.

Section 7

Appendix

The rescue loop (corrected model)

1 · PostDonor posts surplus food
2 · DispatchStaff build it into a run
3 · PickupDriver claims/is assigned it, collects
4 · DistributeDriver + volunteers deliver
5 · DeliveredReached people in need ✓

A driver collects — by claiming an open pickup or being assigned into a run. Volunteers are never the collector; they ride along or meet the run at the drop to help distribute and confirm it. Staff build runs, adjust them live, and can manually assign a specific driver outside the normal claim flow. Every step is recorded — who did it and when — via an audited status-event trail; nothing is marked delivered without going through the proper stages.

Known limitations

  • Run-stop history has a legacy fallback — a stop with zero recorded status-change events (pre-dating the audit-trail feature) shows a thinner completion-time-only line, no actor, no "from" status. Only affects older runs; avoid using one as your first History demo.
  • Destination active/inactive toggle is edit-only — a new destination is always created active; the toggle only matters once it's already in use, which is exactly its intended scope.
  • "Link a donor to a partner" uses two plain native dropdowns, visually inconsistent with the styled component used elsewhere on the Partners page — cosmetic only.
  • The admin overview dashboard can feel slow to first paint — a deliberate architecture trade-off for this hosting platform, not a data problem.
  • A small dead-code onboarding fallback for admin-invited users remains in the code, though it's unreachable in practice — flagged for a future cleanup, not a functional issue.
